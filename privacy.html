Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Clara Rubi Automation collects, uses, stores, and protects information when users access the service, website, or connected platform integrations.

1. Information we collect

  • Account and contact details provided by the user.
  • Media files, captions, schedules, and workflow metadata uploaded by the user.
  • Platform authorization data required to connect supported services.
  • Basic technical logs used for security, diagnostics, and delivery tracking.

2. How we use information

  • To provide media preparation and publishing support requested by the user.
  • To maintain queue status, draft packaging, and posting records.
  • To secure the service, prevent misuse, and investigate errors.
  • To comply with legal obligations and valid platform requirements.

3. Legal and platform compliance

Users are responsible for ensuring that they have the rights and permissions needed for any content uploaded or distributed through the service. The service must not be used to violate privacy rights, intellectual property rights, or platform rules.

4. Age-restricted and sensitive content

The service is intended only for adults. If a user manages age-restricted destinations or premium platforms, they must ensure all related activity complies with local law, age-gating requirements, and the policies of each platform used.

5. Sharing of information

We may share information with service providers, hosting vendors, analytics tools, and platform partners only as needed to operate the service or complete user-requested actions. We may also disclose information when required by law or to protect the safety, rights, or integrity of the service.

6. Data retention

We keep information only for as long as reasonably necessary to operate the service, maintain security records, comply with legal obligations, and resolve disputes.

7. User rights

Depending on the user's location, the user may request access, correction, deletion, or export of certain personal information, subject to applicable law and technical limits.

8. Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures to protect data. No system is completely secure, and users should also protect their credentials and connected accounts.
